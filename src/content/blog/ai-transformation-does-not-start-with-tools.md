Many companies begin their AI work by asking which tools they should buy. That is understandable, but it is usually the wrong starting point.

AI transformation starts with business priorities, workflow clarity, data readiness, and governance. Without those pieces, tool selection becomes an expensive guessing exercise.

## The tool is rarely the first problem

When leaders investigate AI opportunities, the same operational issues often surface:

- Work moves through disconnected systems.
- Employees rely on spreadsheets and email to bridge process gaps.
- Reporting requires manual cleanup.
- Teams are experimenting with AI without shared guidance.
- Leaders are unsure which opportunities deserve funding.

Those are not tool-selection issues. They are operating model issues.

## Start with readiness

Before investing in another platform, leaders should understand where the business is ready, where the foundation is weak, and which opportunities have the strongest business case.

A readiness-first approach helps answer better questions:

- Which workflows create the most manual drag?
- Which systems are limiting visibility?
- Which data sources are reliable enough to support AI?
- Which opportunities are tied to measurable business value?
- What governance or adoption risks need attention first?

## Strategy before software

The strongest AI initiatives are not the flashiest. They are the ones connected to real business priorities, validated operational friction, and practical implementation paths.

That is why modus6 starts with Priority Over Hype: understand the business, evaluate readiness, prioritize the right opportunities, and then decide what to build or buy.
